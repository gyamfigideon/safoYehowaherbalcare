DISEASES TREATED HERE

PROSTATE ENLOARGMENT

The cause of prostate enlargment is unknown, but it's believed to be linked to hormonal changes as a man gets older. The balance of hormones in your body changes as you get older and this may cause your prostate gland to grow.

STROKE INFECTION

There are two main causes of stroke:a blocked artery(ischemic stroke) or leaking or bursting of a blood vessel (hemorrhagic) stroke. Some people temporary disruption of blood flow to the brain, known as a transient ischemic attack (TIA), that doesn't cause lasting symptom.

MENSTRUAL PAIN

During the menstrual period, a chemical called prostaglandins makes the uterus contract to expel its lining. Higher levels of prostaglandins make uterus contract more strongly and can press against the nearby blood vessels, which cuts off the oxygen supply to the tissue and result in pain and cramping.

BOIL INFECTION
